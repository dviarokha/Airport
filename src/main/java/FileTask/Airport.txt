Wrocław Nicolaus Copernicus Airport (IATA: WRO) is an international airport located approximately 10 kilometers southwest of the city center of Wrocław, Poland. It is one of the largest regional airports in the country and serves as an important transportation hub for the Lower Silesian Voivodeship.
The airport is named after Nicolaus Copernicus, the renowned astronomer who was born in the region. The modern passenger terminal, opened in 2012, is equipped with state-of-the-art facilities, including domestic and international flight zones, duty-free shops, restaurants, waiting areas, and a business lounge.
Wrocław Airport handles both regular and seasonal flights. Airlines operating at the airport include Ryanair, Wizz Air, Lufthansa, LOT, and others. Popular destinations include London, Frankfurt, Oslo, Dublin, Munich, Milan, and holiday resorts in Spain, Greece, and Egypt.
Thanks to its convenient transportation links, high level of service, and steady passenger growth, Wrocław Airport plays a key role in Poland’s aviation infrastructure.
